### The STAR Method
- **S**ituation - Set the scene
- **T**ask - What needs to be proven
- **A**ction - What evidence will show
- **R**esult - What you want the court to do

### Powerful Opening Techniques

#### Hook the Judge/Jury
1. **Start with Impact**
   - "This case is about broken promises."
   - "On [date], defendant's negligence changed my client's life forever."
   - "The evidence will show that defendant knowingly deceived my client."

2. **Use Active Voice**
   - ‚ùå "Mistakes were made"
   - ‚úÖ "Defendant made critical errors"

3. **Paint the Picture**
   - Include sensory details
   - Make it relatable
   - Create emotional connection

#### Opening Statement Structure
```
1. Introduction (30 seconds)
2. What happened (3-5 minutes)
3. What the evidence will show (2-3 minutes)
4. What we're asking for (1 minute)
5. Conclusion/Theme (30 seconds)
```

## ‚öñÔ∏è EXAMINATION STRATEGIES

### Direct Examination Mastery

#### The W Questions Framework
- **Who** was involved?
- **What** happened?
- **When** did it occur?
- **Where** did it take place?
- **Why** is it important?

#### Advanced Techniques

1. **Primacy/Recency Effect**
   - Start strong with best evidence
   - End strong with memorable point
   - Bury weaknesses in the middle

2. **Show, Don't Tell**
   - Use exhibits and demonstratives
   - Let witness describe what they see
   - Make it visual and memorable

3. **Emotional Moments**
   - Slow down for impact
   - Use silence effectively
   - Allow natural emotion

### Cross-Examination Warfare

#### The CRAFT Method Expanded
- **C**ontrol through leading questions
- **R**epeat their damaging admissions
- **A**ttack when you have ammunition
- **F**ocus on one point at a time
- **T**erminate when you're ahead

#### Advanced Cross Techniques

1. **The Rule of Three**
   - Make your point three different ways
   - Three is memorable and persuasive
   - Stop at three - don't over-argue

2. **Incremental Commitment**
   - Start with easy admissions
   - Build to the difficult ones
   - Trap them in their logic

3. **The Zinger Finish**
   - End with your best point
   - Make it quotable
   - Leave the judge/jury thinking

## üõ°Ô∏è DEFENSIVE STRATEGIES

### Dealing with Bad Facts

#### The ADMIT Strategy
- **A**cknowledge the bad fact
- **D**iminish its importance
- **M**inimize the damage
- **I**ntegrate into your narrative
- **T**ransition to good facts

#### Example Application
"Yes, my client was late with the payment. But this was the first time in three years of timely payments, and it was only three days late due to a banking error that was immediately corrected."

### Handling Difficult Witnesses

#### Hostile Witnesses
1. **Stay Calm**
   - Don't take the bait
   - Maintain professionalism
   - Use their hostility against them

2. **Control the Pace**
   - Short, simple questions
   - One fact per question
   - Don't let them ramble

3. **Focus on Facts**
   - Avoid opinions
   - Stick to what they saw/heard
   - Use documents when possible

#### Evasive Witnesses
1. **Pin Them Down**
   - Repeat the question
   - Ask for yes/no answers
   - Use their evasion against them

2. **Use Their Own Words**
   - Quote from depositions
   - Cite their documents
   - Make them explain contradictions

- **I**dentification - Mark it properly
- **R**elevance - Connect to your case

#### Foundation Questions
1. "Do you recognize this document?"
2. "Have you seen it before?"
3. "What is it?"
4. "How do you recognize it?"
5. "Is this a true and accurate copy?"

#### Authentication Techniques

- Chain of custody
- Business records exception

- Expert testimony

### Structure That Wins

#### The POWER Framework
- **P**roblem - What happened
- **O**pportunity - What could have been done
- **W**itness - What the evidence shows
- **E**motion - Why it matters
- **R**esolution - What you want

### Persuasion Techniques

#### Logical Arguments
1. **Syllogism Structure**
   - Major premise (law)
   - Minor premise (facts)
   - Conclusion (result)

2. **Burden of Proof**
   - Remind court of standard
   - Show how you've met it
   - Point out opponent's failures

#### Emotional Appeals
1. **Justice Themes**
   - Fairness and accountability
   - Right vs. wrong
   - Consequences of inaction

2. **Human Impact**
   - Real-world effects
   - Future implications
   - Moral obligations

## üé™ PSYCHOLOGICAL WARFARE

### Reading the Room

#### Judge Signals
- **Engaged:** Leaning forward, taking notes
- **Bored:** Looking away, checking time
- **Annoyed:** Interrupting, sharp questions
- **Convinced:** Nodding, asking clarifying questions

#### Jury Signals (if applicable)
- **Interested:** Eye contact, alert posture
- **Confused:** Puzzled expressions, looking away
- **Sympathetic:** Nodding, emotional reactions
- **Hostile:** Arms crossed, negative expressions

### Tactical Adjustments

#### When You're Winning
- Don't over-argue
- Stay focused on theme
- End on high notes
- Avoid unnecessary risks

#### When You're Losing
- Acknowledge weaknesses early
- Focus on remaining strengths
- Look for opponent mistakes
- Consider settlement opportunities
