### Direct Examination - Tenant (Edith)

#### Housing Search & Agreement Formation
1. "How did you first learn about this rental property?"
2. "When did you first view the apartment/house?"
3. "Who showed you the property?"
4. "What was the condition of the property when you first saw it?"
5. "Were there any problems you noticed during the initial viewing?"
6. "Did you point out any issues to the landlord or agent?"
7. "What was their response?"
8. "When did you decide to rent the property?"
9. "What was the agreed-upon rent amount?"
10. "What was the security deposit amount?"

#### Lease Terms & Move-In
11. "Did you receive a written lease agreement?"
12. "Did you read the lease before signing?"
13. "Were any verbal agreements made that weren't in the written lease?"
14. "When was your move-in date?"
15. "Did you conduct a move-in inspection?"
16. "Did you document any existing damage or problems?"
17. "Did you take photographs of the property's condition?"
18. "Were utilities included in the rent?"
19. "What furnishings or appliances were included?"
20. "Did you receive all keys and access codes?"

#### Tenancy Issues
21. "How long did you live at the property?"
22. "Did you always pay rent on time?"
23. "What was your typical method of paying rent?"
24. "Did you ever receive notices about late rent?"
25. "When did you first notice [specific problem - repairs needed, habitability issues, etc.]?"
26. "How did this problem affect your use and enjoyment of the property?"
27. "Did you notify the landlord about this issue?"
28. "How did you notify them - phone, email, text, written notice?"
29. "What was the landlord's response?"
30. "How long did the problem persist?"

#### Repair Issues & Habitability
31. "What specific repairs were needed?"
32. "Were these emergency repairs or routine maintenance?"
33. "Did the problems affect the habitability of the property?"
34. "Were you able to use all areas of the property normally?"
35. "Did you have to relocate temporarily due to any issues?"
36. "Did you incur any additional expenses because of these problems?"
37. "Did you hire anyone to make repairs yourself?"
38. "Did you withhold rent due to repair issues?"
39. "Were there any safety concerns?"
40. "Did you contact any government agencies about the problems?"

#### Move-Out & Security Deposit
41. "When did you give notice that you were moving out?"
42. "How much notice did you provide?"
43. "What was required under your lease for notice?"
44. "Did you clean the property before moving out?"
45. "Did you conduct a move-out inspection?"
46. "What was the condition of the property when you left?"
47. "Did you cause any damage to the property?"
48. "When did you expect to receive your security deposit back?"
49. "Did you receive your security deposit?"
50. "What deductions, if any, were made from your deposit?"

#### Damages & Financial Impact
51. "What financial losses have you suffered?"
52. "Did you have to pay for temporary housing?"
53. "Did you lose any personal property?"
54. "Did you incur moving expenses due to the problems?"
55. "Do you have receipts for your out-of-pocket expenses?"

### Cross-Examination - Landlord

#### Lease Obligations
56. "You're familiar with Minnesota landlord-tenant law, aren't you?"
57. "You understand your obligation to maintain the property in habitable condition?"
58. "You received notice of the problem on [date], correct?"
59. "You had a duty to make repairs within a reasonable time, didn't you?"
60. "You failed to make these repairs for [time period], correct?"

#### Notice & Response
61. "Tenant notified you of this problem, didn't they?"
62. "You received their email/text/call on [date], right?"
63. "You understood that this was affecting their ability to live in the property?"
64. "You promised to fix the problem, didn't you?"
65. "You failed to keep that promise, correct?"

#### Financial Issues
66. "You continued to collect full rent despite these problems?"
67. "You knew the property wasn't fully habitable, didn't you?"
68. "You kept tenant's full security deposit, correct?"
69. "You didn't provide an itemized list of deductions, did you?"
70. "These deductions weren't for normal wear and tear, were they?"

### Potential Claims
1. **Security Deposit Return**
   - Improper deductions
   - Failure to provide itemized statement
   - Bad faith retention

2. **Breach of Warranty of Habitability**
   - Failure to maintain property
   - Unsafe/unsanitary conditions
   - Interference with quiet enjoyment

3. **Damages**
   - Out-of-pocket expenses
   - Temporary housing costs
   - Moving expenses
   - Rent reduction/refund

### Minnesota Law Requirements
- **Security Deposit:** Must be returned within 21 days with itemized deductions
- **Habitability:** Landlord must maintain premises in habitable condition
- **Notice:** Tenant must provide reasonable notice of problems
- **Remedies:** Rent withholding, repair and deduct, damages
