## DIRECT EXAMINATION OUTLINE

### Phase 1: Foundation & Credibility (5-10 minutes)

#### Personal Background
1. **Name & Address**
   - "Please state your full name for the record."
   - "What is your current address?"
   - "How long have you lived there?"

2. **Professional Background**
   - "What is your occupation?"
   - "How long have you worked in that field?"
   - "What are your primary responsibilities?"

3. **Credibility Foundation**
   - "Do you have any interest in the outcome of this case?"
   - "Are you related to any party in this lawsuit?"
   - "Have you ever been convicted of a felony?"

### Phase 2: Knowledge Foundation (10-15 minutes)

#### Connection to Case
4. **Initial Involvement**
   - "How did you become involved in this matter?"
   - "When did you first learn about [incident/contract/dispute]?"
   - "Who else was present when you first became aware of this?"

5. **Relationship to Parties**
   - "How do you know [plaintiff/defendant]?"
   - "How long have you known them?"
   - "What is the nature of your relationship?"

### Phase 3: Substantive Testimony (20-30 minutes)

#### Core Events
6. **Timeline Development**
   - "Let's start at the beginning. When did [first event] occur?"
   - "What were you doing just before [incident]?"
   - "What happened next?"
   - "How much time passed between [event A] and [event B]?"

7. **Detailed Observations**
   - "What did you see?"
   - "What did you hear?"
   - "Who was present?"
   - "What was said?"
   - "How did [person] appear/react?"

8. **Documentation**
   - "Did you make any notes at the time?"
   - "Did you take any photographs?"
   - "Do you have any documents relating to this?"
   - "Have you reviewed any documents to refresh your memory?"

### Phase 4: Damages/Impact (10-15 minutes)

#### Financial Impact
9. **Losses Incurred**
   - "What losses have you suffered as a result?"
   - "How do you calculate that amount?"
   - "Do you have records supporting that calculation?"

10. **Mitigation Efforts**
    - "What steps have you taken to minimize your losses?"
    - "Have you been able to find alternative arrangements?"

## CROSS-EXAMINATION STRATEGY

### Phase 1: Control & Establish Facts (5-10 minutes)

#### Favorable Admissions
1. **Lock in Good Facts**
   - "You would agree that [favorable fact], correct?"
   - "In fact, [favorable fact], isn't that right?"
   - "There's no dispute that [favorable fact], is there?"

### Phase 2: Impeachment (10-20 minutes)

#### Prior Inconsistent Statements
2. **Setup Questions**
   - "You testified today that [current testimony], correct?"
   - "You were under oath when you testified, weren't you?"
   - "You understood the importance of telling the truth?"

3. **Impeachment Sequence**
   - "Do you recall giving a deposition in this case?"
   - "You were also under oath at that time?"
   - "Do you recall being asked [question] at your deposition?"
   - "And do you recall answering [prior answer]?"
   - "That's different from what you testified to today, isn't it?"

#### Bias/Interest
4. **Motivation Questions**
   - "You're being sued by my client, aren't you?"
   - "You stand to gain [amount] if you win this case?"
   - "You've discussed this case with your lawyer?"
   - "Your lawyer prepared you for this testimony?"

### Phase 3: Credibility Challenges (5-15 minutes)

#### Memory Issues
5. **Time/Memory Problems**
   - "This happened [time period] ago, didn't it?"
   - "You didn't write anything down at the time?"
   - "You've talked about this with others since then?"
   - "Your memory could be mistaken, couldn't it?"

#### Character for Truthfulness
6. **Background Questions**
   - "Have you ever been arrested?"
   - "Have you ever been convicted of a crime?"
   - "Have you ever filed a false insurance claim?"
   - "Do you consider yourself an honest person?"

#### Tenant Direct Examination
- "When did you first view the rental property?"
- "What was the condition of the property when you moved in?"

- "You had a duty to respond promptly, didn't you?"
- "You failed to address this issue for [time period], correct?"
- "This violation continued throughout the tenancy, didn't it?"

## OBJECTION QUICK REFERENCE

### Common Objections & Responses

#### "Objection, leading."
- **When to use:** Direct examination using leading questions
- **Response:** "I'll rephrase, Your Honor."

#### "Objection, hearsay."
- **When to use:** Out-of-court statement offered for truth
- **Response:** "Goes to state of mind, not truth of matter asserted."

#### "Objection, relevance."
- **When to use:** Question not related to issues in case
- **Response:** "Goes to credibility, Your Honor."

#### "Objection, speculation."
- **When to use:** Asking witness to guess or assume
- **Response:** "Based on witness's personal knowledge."

#### "Objection, argumentative."
- **When to use:** Attorney arguing with witness
- **Response:** "I'll move on, Your Honor."
