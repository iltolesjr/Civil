# Plain-Language Legal Glossary

- Affidavit: A written statement of facts you swear are true before a notary.
- Statement of Claims (Complaint): The document that starts a case and states what happened, the law, and what you want the court to order.
- Venue: The correct court location for the case (often the county where events occurred).
- Jurisdiction: The court’s power to hear the case and make binding decisions.
- Service of Process: Official delivery of the lawsuit to the other side so they’re notified.
- Exhibit: A labeled piece of evidence (photo, email, receipt) referenced in filings.
- Retaliation: Punishment because you exercised a legal right (e.g., requesting repairs, checking mail).
- Prima Facie: The basic set of facts that, if true, satisfy the elements of a claim.
- Chain of Custody: Record of who handled evidence and when, to show it hasn’t been altered.
- Demand Letter: A pre-filing letter asking for a specific fix; shows you tried to resolve it informally.
- Discovery: Exchange of information/evidence after filing (may be limited in small claims).
- Relief/Remedy: What you ask the court to order (return property, money, stop conduct).
- Statute: A written law passed by the legislature (e.g., Minn. Stat. § 504B.441).
- Damages: Money or other losses you seek.
- Elements (of a claim): The required parts you must prove to win that claim.
- Adverse Action: A negative action taken against you (e.g., threats, property seizure) after protected activity.
- Protected Activity: A legally protected step (e.g., asserting rights, making a complaint).
