# Filing Options Guide - Where to File Your Complaint

## Overview
This guide explains the different places where you can file a complaint about housing, landlord, or civil disputes. Choose the right venue based on your specific situation and the relief you're seeking.

---

## 1. Conciliation Court (Small Claims Court)

### What It Is
Minnesota's small claims court for disputes involving money up to $15,000. It's faster, cheaper, and more informal than regular court.

### When to Use
- Seeking money damages under $15,000
- Security deposit disputes
- Unpaid rent claims (if you're the landlord)
- Damage to property
- Breach of contract
- Return of personal property worth less than $15,000

### How to File
**Location**: County courthouse where the defendant lives or where the issue occurred

**Filing Fee**: $50-$100 (depending on claim amount)
- Under $2,500: $50
- $2,500-$7,500: $75
- $7,500-$15,000: $100
- **Fee waiver available**: File "Affidavit of Inability to Pay"

**Required Documents**:
- Statement of Claim form
- Summons
- Affidavit of Service (after serving defendant)
- Supporting evidence and documents

**Process**:
1. Complete Statement of Claim form (available at courthouse or online)
2. File at courthouse and pay fee (or request waiver)
3. Serve the defendant (sheriff, process server, or certified mail)
4. Attend court hearing
5. Judge makes decision immediately or within 30 days

**Pros**:
- ✅ Relatively quick (typically 30-90 days)
- ✅ Low or no filing fees
- ✅ Informal process - lawyers not required
- ✅ No need for extensive legal knowledge
- ✅ Can represent yourself (pro se)

**Cons**:
- ❌ Limited to $15,000 maximum
- ❌ Limited discovery (pre-trial evidence gathering)
- ❌ Can't get injunctions or orders (only money)
- ❌ May still need to enforce the judgment yourself

**Contact**: Your county courthouse
- Hennepin County: (612) 348-3526
- Ramsey County: (651) 266-8200
- Find your county: www.mncourts.gov

## 2. District Court (Regular Civil Court)

### What It Is
The general court for civil lawsuits with no dollar limit, can grant various types of relief.

### When to Use
- Claims over $15,000
- Seeking an injunction or restraining order
- Eviction proceedings (Housing Court division)
- Complex legal issues
- Need for extensive discovery
- Seeking non-monetary relief

### How to File
**Location**: County district court

**Filing Fee**: Approximately $300-$400
- Fee waiver available with Affidavit of Inability to Pay

**Required Documents**:
- Summons and Complaint
- Civil Cover Sheet
- Supporting affidavits
- Certificate of representation (if pro se)

**Process**:
1. Draft and file Summons and Complaint
2. Pay filing fee or request waiver
3. Serve the defendant
4. Defendant has 20 days to answer
5. Discovery period (can take months)
6. Possible mediation
7. Trial or settlement
8. Judgment

**Pros**:
- ✅ No dollar limit on claims
- ✅ Can get injunctions and court orders
- ✅ Full discovery process available
- ✅ Can address complex legal issues
- ✅ Greater enforceability

**Cons**:
- ❌ More expensive (higher fees, may need lawyer)
- ❌ Much slower (can take 6-18 months)
- ❌ More complex procedures
- ❌ Harder to represent yourself
- ❌ May require extensive legal knowledge

**Contact**: Your county district court
- Same locations as conciliation court
- www.mncourts.gov

## 3. Minnesota Attorney General's Office

### What It Is
State agency that handles consumer protection and can investigate businesses.

### When to Use
- Landlord is a large company/corporation
- Pattern of violations affecting multiple tenants
- Consumer fraud or deceptive practices
- Predatory lending
- Scams or unfair business practices
- Violations of state consumer protection laws

### How to File
**Location**: File online or by mail

**Filing Fee**: FREE

**Required Information**:
- Your contact information
- Business/landlord information
- Description of the problem
- Supporting documentation
- What you want to happen

**Process**:
1. Submit complaint online at www.ag.state.mn.us
2. AG reviews complaint
3. May investigate if pattern of violations
4. May mediate between parties
5. May take enforcement action against business

**Pros**:
- ✅ Completely free
- ✅ AG has legal authority and resources
- ✅ Can result in industry-wide changes
- ✅ May pressure defendant to settle
- ✅ No court appearance required

**Cons**:
- ❌ AG won't represent you personally
- ❌ Won't get you money directly
- ❌ AG may not take action on individual cases
- ❌ Slow process
- ❌ No guaranteed outcome

**Contact**:
- Website: www.ag.state.mn.us/consumer
- Phone: (651) 296-3353 or (800) 657-3787
- Address: 445 Minnesota Street, Suite 1200, St. Paul, MN 55101

## 4. Minnesota Department of Health (MDH)

### What It Is
State agency responsible for health and safety standards in rental housing.

### When to Use
- Health hazards (mold, lead paint, pests)
- Unsafe living conditions
- No heat, water, or essential utilities
- Structural hazards
- Sewage or sanitation issues
- Code violations affecting health/safety

### How to File
**Location**: File complaint with local health department or MDH

**Filing Fee**: FREE

**Required Information**:
- Property address
- Description of health/safety issues
- Photos if available
- Landlord information
- How long the problem has existed

**Process**:
1. File complaint with local health department
2. Inspector visits property
3. Inspector documents violations
4. Landlord receives order to correct violations
5. Follow-up inspection
6. Fines or legal action if not corrected

**Pros**:
- ✅ Free inspection
- ✅ Official documentation of violations
- ✅ Can force landlord to make repairs
- ✅ Creates official record
- ✅ May result in penalties for landlord

**Cons**:
- ❌ Doesn't get you money damages
- ❌ Repairs may take time
- ❌ May need to follow up with court case
- ❌ Possible landlord retaliation (illegal but happens)

**Contact**:
- Minneapolis: (311) or (612) 673-3000
- St. Paul: (651) 266-9090
- MDH General: (651) 201-5000 or (888) 345-0823
- Find your local health department: www.health.state.mn.us

## 5. HUD (U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development)

### What It Is
Federal agency that enforces fair housing laws and handles discrimination complaints.

### When to Use
- Housing discrimination based on:
  - Race, color, national origin
  - Religion
  - Sex (including sexual harassment)
  - Disability
  - Familial status (children)
- Refusal to make reasonable accommodations
- Discriminatory advertising
- Discriminatory terms or conditions
- Retaliation for asserting fair housing rights

### How to File
**Location**: File online, by mail, or phone

**Filing Fee**: FREE

**Time Limit**: Must file within 1 year of discrimination

**Required Information**:
- Your information
- Property/landlord information
- Details of discrimination
- When it occurred
- Witnesses or evidence

**Process**:
1. File complaint with HUD
2. HUD investigates (100 days)
3. Possible mediation
4. If violation found, HUD may:
   - Issue charge
   - File lawsuit
   - Seek damages and penalties

**Pros**:
- ✅ Free federal investigation
- ✅ HUD attorneys handle case
- ✅ Can get significant damages
- ✅ Strong enforcement power
- ✅ Creates important precedent

**Cons**:
- ❌ Only for discrimination cases
- ❌ Investigation takes time
- ❌ May not take all cases
- ❌ Must file within 1 year

**Contact**:
- Online: www.hud.gov/fairhousing
- Phone: (800) 669-9777
- Minneapolis HUD Office: (612) 370-3000
- Address: 330 Second Ave S #240, Minneapolis, MN 55401

## 6. Minnesota Department of Human Rights

### What It Is
State agency that enforces Minnesota Human Rights Act (broader than federal law).

### When to Use
- Same as HUD (discrimination)
- PLUS discrimination based on:
  - Public assistance status
  - Sexual orientation
  - Gender identity
  - Marital status
  - Local human rights ordinances

### How to File
**Location**: File online or by mail

**Filing Fee**: FREE

**Time Limit**: 1 year from discrimination

**Process**: Similar to HUD

**Pros**:
- ✅ Broader protections than federal law
- ✅ Free investigation
- ✅ State enforcement
- ✅ Can file with both HUD and MDHR

**Cons**:
- ❌ Only for discrimination
- ❌ Takes time
- ❌ No guaranteed outcome

**Contact**:
- Website: mn.gov/mdhr
- Phone: (651) 539-1100 or (800) 657-3704
- Address: 625 Robert Street North, St. Paul, MN 55155

## 7. City/County Housing Inspection Department

### What It Is
Local government office that enforces housing codes.

### When to Use
- Code violations
- Licensing issues
- Rental registration violations
- Local ordinance violations
- Property maintenance issues

### How to File
**Location**: City or county office

**Filing Fee**: FREE

**Process**:
1. File complaint
2. Inspector visits
3. Issues citations if violations found
4. Landlord must correct or face fines

**Pros**:
- ✅ Free
- ✅ Local enforcement
- ✅ Creates official record
- ✅ May force repairs

**Cons**:
- ❌ Doesn't get you money
- ❌ Limited to code violations

**Contact**:
- Minneapolis: (612) 673-5858
- St. Paul: (651) 266-8989
- Your city's website

## 8. Better Business Bureau (BBB)

### What It Is
Private organization that tracks business complaints.

### When to Use
- Supplemental to other actions
- Creating record of complaints
- Large property management companies
- Consumer issues

### How to File
**Location**: www.bbb.org

**Filing Fee**: FREE

**Process**:
1. File complaint online
2. BBB contacts business
3. Business responds
4. May facilitate resolution

**Pros**:
- ✅ Free and easy
- ✅ May motivate business to respond
- ✅ Creates public record
- ✅ Affects business rating

**Cons**:
- ❌ No enforcement power
- ❌ Business doesn't have to respond
- ❌ Not effective for small landlords
- ❌ Can't get money or force action

**Contact**: www.bbb.org

- Serves: 33 counties in southern Minnesota

**Legal Aid Service of Northeastern Minnesota**
- Phone: (800) 622-7266

- Serves: Northeastern Minnesota

**Volunteer Lawyers Network**
- Phone: (612) 752-6677

- ❌ May not take all case types

### What It Is
Service that connects you with private attorneys.

### When to Use
- Don't qualify for legal aid
- Need legal representation
- Can afford to pay
- Complex case

### How to Use
**Contact**: (612) 333-1183 or (800) 292-4152
**Website**: www.mnbar.org

**Process**:
1. Call referral service
2. Brief screening
3. Referred to attorney in your area
4. First consultation: $50 for 30 minutes
5. Decide whether to hire attorney

**Pros**:
- ✅ Find qualified lawyer
- ✅ Low-cost initial consultation
- ✅ Lawyers experienced in area you need

**Cons**:
- ❌ Must pay for representation
- ❌ Attorney fees can be expensive

## 12. Mediation Services

### What It Is
Neutral third party helps you and landlord reach agreement.

### When to Use
- Both parties willing to negotiate
- Want to avoid court
- Preserve relationship
- Faster resolution

### Services**:

**Statewide ADR (Alternative Dispute Resolution)**
- Court-connected mediation services
- Contact your county courthouse

**Community Mediation Services**
- Various organizations throughout Minnesota
- Often free or low cost

**Process**:
1. Both parties agree to mediate
2. Meet with mediator
3. Discuss issues
4. Negotiate settlement
5. Sign agreement if reached

**Pros**:
- ✅ Faster than court
- ✅ Less expensive
- ✅ Less adversarial
- ✅ You control the outcome
- ✅ Can preserve relationships

**Cons**:
- ❌ Both parties must agree
- ❌ Not binding unless you make it so
- ❌ Landlord may not cooperate
- ❌ May not work for severe violations

## Quick Decision Matrix

### Choose Conciliation Court If:
- ✅ Seeking money damages under $15,000
- ✅ Want relatively quick resolution
- ✅ Clear-cut case
- ✅ Don't want to hire lawyer

### Choose District Court If:
- ✅ Claim over $15,000
- ✅ Need injunction or court order
- ✅ Complex legal issues
- ✅ Need extensive discovery

### File with Attorney General If:
- ✅ Large corporation landlord
- ✅ Pattern affecting multiple people
- ✅ Consumer fraud involved
- ✅ Want systemic change

### File with Health Department If:
- ✅ Health or safety violations
- ✅ Need official inspection
- ✅ Want landlord forced to repair
- ✅ Building code violations

### File with HUD/MDHR If:
- ✅ Discrimination occurred
- ✅ Protected class involved
- ✅ Want federal/state investigation
- ✅ Retaliation for asserting rights

### Contact Legal Aid If:
- ✅ Low income
- ✅ Need lawyer representation
- ✅ Facing eviction
- ✅ Complex legal issues

## Can You File Multiple Complaints?

**YES!** You can and often should file in multiple places:

### Common Combinations:
1. **Conciliation Court + Health Department**
   - Health dept forces repairs
   - Court gets you money damages

2. **Conciliation Court + Attorney General**
   - AG investigates company
   - Court gets you compensation

3. **HUD + Conciliation Court**
   - HUD handles discrimination
   - Court handles other damages

4. **Health Department + District Court**
   - Health creates evidence
   - Use in court case

### Important Notes:
- ⚠️ You can only get money once (no double recovery)
- ⚠️ Filing deadlines differ by venue
- ⚠️ Some venues may defer to others
- ⚠️ Coordinate your filings strategically

## Filing Deadlines to Remember

| Venue | Deadline | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Conciliation Court | Generally 6 years | Breach of contract |
| District Court | Varies by claim type | 2-6 years typically |
| HUD Discrimination | 1 year | From discriminatory act |
| MDHR Discrimination | 1 year | From discriminatory act |
| Attorney General | No strict deadline | File ASAP |
| Health Department | No strict deadline | File while you're still tenant |

**⚠️ WARNING**: Don't wait! File as soon as possible. Evidence gets lost, memories fade, and deadlines can bar your claims.
