## Tips for Collecting Witness Statements

### Finding Witnesses
- Neighbors who saw or heard issues
- Other tenants with similar problems
- Friends/family who visited and saw conditions
- Repair persons or service providers
- Health inspectors or code enforcement officers
- Police officers who responded to calls
- Delivery persons or mail carriers
- Anyone who observed the situation

### Approaching Potential Witnesses
- Explain the situation briefly
- Ask if they're willing to help
- Emphasize they just need to tell the truth
- Assure them it's voluntary
- Explain they may need to appear in court
- Thank them for their time

### Getting Good Statements
- Have them write in their own words
- Don't coach or lead them
- Ask them to be specific (dates, times, details)
- Let them describe what they personally witnessed
- Don't ask them to guess or speculate
- Have them sign and date the statement
- Get contact information
- Make a copy for your records

### What Makes a Strong Witness Statement
- ✅ Personal knowledge (not hearsay)
- ✅ Specific dates and times
- ✅ Detailed descriptions
- ✅ Directly relevant to your claims
- ✅ Consistent with other evidence
- ✅ Written in witness's own words
- ✅ Signed and dated
- ✅ Witness willing to testify

### What to Avoid
- ❌ Statements that are just opinion
- ❌ "I heard from someone that..."
- ❌ Vague or general statements
- ❌ Irrelevant information
- ❌ Coaching the witness
- ❌ Having witness exaggerate
- ❌ Unsigned statements
- ❌ Lost contact information

## Witness Preparation Checklist

**Before Trial**:
- [ ] Contact witness to confirm attendance
- [ ] Provide court date, time, and location
- [ ] Review what they'll be asked about
- [ ] Explain court procedures
- [ ] Remind them to dress professionally
- [ ] Arrange subpoena if needed
- [ ] Confirm contact information still valid
- [ ] Remind them to tell the truth
- [ ] Let them know they can say "I don't know"
- [ ] Thank them for their help

**Day of Trial**:
- [ ] Remind witness of time/location
- [ ] Ensure they know where to park
- [ ] Meet them before court starts
- [ ] Review their statement briefly
- [ ] Introduce them to you (if not already known)
- [ ] Explain what to expect
- [ ] Have them wait in designated area
- [ ] Call them when needed

## Sample Witness Questions for Trial

**Establishing Credibility**:
1. Please state your name for the record.
2. Where do you live?
3. How do you know Jeff Mills?
4. How long have you known him?

**Getting to the Point**:
5. Were you present at [location] on [date]?
6. What did you observe?
7. Can you describe what you saw in detail?
8. What time was this?
9. Who else was present?

**Connecting to Evidence**:
10. I'm showing you what's been marked as Exhibit [X]. Do you recognize this?
11. Is this what you saw on that day?
12. Is this a fair and accurate representation?

**Establishing Relevance**:
13. How did what you witnessed affect Jeff Mills?
14. Did you see the condition before and after?
15. Was this a one-time event or did it happen multiple times?

---

**Remember**: Witnesses should only testify to what they personally saw, heard, or experienced. They should not guess, speculate, or repeat what others told them (hearsay).
