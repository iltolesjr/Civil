## DIRECT EXAMINATION QUESTIONS

### ğŸ¯ Foundation Building Questions

#### Personal Background
1. "Please state your full name for the record."
2. "What is your current address?"
3. "How long have you lived at that address?"
4. "What is your occupation?"
5. "How long have you been employed in that capacity?"

#### Establishing Credibility
6. "Do you have any training or experience relevant to this matter?"
7. "Have you ever been convicted of a felony?"
8. "Do you have any interest in the outcome of this case?"
9. "Are you being compensated for your testimony today?"
10. "Have you discussed your testimony with anyone?"

### ğŸ“… Timeline Development Questions

#### Event Sequence
11. "Let's start at the beginning. When did you first become aware of [incident]?"
12. "What were you doing immediately before [event]?"
13. "Who else was present at that time?"
14. "What happened next?"
15. "How much time passed between [event A] and [event B]?"

#### Sensory Details
16. "What did you see?"
17. "What did you hear?"
18. "Did you notice any smells?"
19. "What was the lighting like?"
20. "How was the weather?"

#### Performance
26. "What were your obligations under the agreement?"

## CROSS-EXAMINATION STRATEGIES

### âš”ï¸ The CRAFT Method
- **C**ontrol the witness
- **R**epeat favorable testimony
- **A**ttack credibility when necessary
- **F**ocus on inconsistencies
- **T**erminate on a high note

### ğŸª Leading Question Techniques

#### Impeachment Questions
1. "You testified on direct examination that [X], correct?"
2. "But that's not what you said in your deposition, is it?"
3. "In fact, you testified under oath that [Y], didn't you?"
4. "Were you lying then or are you lying now?"
5. "You've changed your story, haven't you?"

#### Bias/Interest Questions
6. "You're being sued by my client, aren't you?"
7. "You stand to gain financially if you win this case?"
8. "You've discussed this case with your attorney?"
9. "Your attorney told you what to say, didn't he?"
10. "You have a personal relationship with the plaintiff?"

#### Memory/Perception Challenges
11. "This incident happened over [time period] ago?"
12. "You didn't write anything down at the time?"
13. "You've discussed this with others since then?"
14. "Your memory could be mistaken, couldn't it?"
15. "You weren't paying close attention, were you?"

### ğŸ” Specific Impeachment Tactics

#### Prior Inconsistent Statements
- Always follow the Rule of Three:
  1. Lock them into the current testimony
  2. Show the prior inconsistent statement
  3. Confront them with the inconsistency

#### Character for Truthfulness
16. "Have you ever been arrested?"
17. "Have you ever been convicted of a crime involving dishonesty?"
18. "Have you ever filed a false police report?"
19. "Have you ever lied on a job application?"
20. "Do you consider yourself an honest person?"

## WITNESS PREPARATION

### ğŸ“ Witness Interview Checklist

#### Initial Contact
- [ ] Explain your role and the case
- [ ] Discuss attorney-client privilege (if applicable)
- [ ] Obtain contact information
- [ ] Schedule formal interview

#### Substantive Interview
- [ ] Record the interview (with permission)
- [ ] Ask open-ended questions first
- [ ] Use conversation templates from case files
- [ ] Document exact quotes
- [ ] Identify supporting documents

#### Pre-Testimony Preparation
- [ ] Review testimony outline
- [ ] Practice direct examination
- [ ] Anticipate cross-examination
- [ ] Discuss courtroom procedures
- [ ] Address nervousness/anxiety

### ğŸ­ Witness Coaching Do's and Don'ts

#### DO:
- Explain the legal process
- Review documents to refresh memory
- Practice speaking clearly and slowly
- Advise to pause before answering
- Remind to say "I don't know" if uncertain

#### DON'T:
- Tell them what to say
- Coach them to lie
- Discuss privileged information inappropriately
- Meet with opposing party's witnesses
- Allow ex parte contact with represented parties

## COURTROOM TIPS AND TRICKS

### ğŸ›ï¸ Courtroom Etiquette

#### Before Court
- [ ] Arrive 30 minutes early
- [ ] Dress professionally
- [ ] Organize materials systematically
- [ ] Test any technology
- [ ] Review opening statement

#### During Proceedings
- [ ] Stand when addressing the court
- [ ] Address the judge as "Your Honor"
- [ ] Never interrupt the judge
- [ ] Maintain eye contact with witnesses
- [ ] Use confident body language

#### Objection Practice
**Common Objections:**
- "Objection, leading."
- "Objection, calls for speculation."
- "Objection, hearsay."
- "Objection, relevance."
- "Objection, asked and answered."

### ğŸ’¡ Strategic Presentation Tips

#### Opening Statement Strategy
1. **Tell a story** - Make it memorable
2. **Start strong** - First impression matters
3. **Preview evidence** - What will you prove?
4. **Address weaknesses** - Control the narrative
5. **End with theme** - Reinforce your message

#### Closing Argument Power
1. **Summarize evidence** - What did you prove?
2. **Address credibility** - Who should be believed?
3. **Connect to law** - Why should you win?
4. **Call for action** - What do you want?
5. **End memorably** - Leave lasting impression

## CASE-SPECIFIC QUESTION BANKS

### ğŸ  Contract/Rental Disputes (Edith Case Style)

#### Landlord-Tenant Questions
1. "When did you first enter into the rental agreement?"
2. "What was the agreed-upon rent amount?"
3. "When was rent due each month?"
4. "Did you ever receive notice of rent increases?"
5. "What was the condition of the property when you moved in?"
6. "Did you document any existing damage?"
7. "What repairs did you request during tenancy?"
8. "How did the landlord respond to repair requests?"
9. "When did you provide notice to vacate?"
10. "What was the condition when you moved out?"

#### Security Deposit Issues
11. "How much was your security deposit?"
12. "When did you pay the security deposit?"
13. "Did you receive a receipt for the deposit?"
14. "What deductions were claimed by the landlord?"
15. "Do you dispute any of these deductions?"

### ğŸ¢ Business/Commercial Disputes (Concepts26 Style)

#### Contract Formation
16. "How did you first learn about defendant's business?"
17. "What services were you seeking?"
18. "Who did you initially speak with?"
19. "What representations were made about the services?"
20. "When was the contract signed?"

#### Performance Issues
21. "What was the agreed-upon timeline?"
22. "Were there any milestone dates?"
23. "When did you first notice problems?"
24. "How did you communicate your concerns?"
25. "What response did you receive?"

#### Damages Assessment
26. "What additional costs did you incur?"
27. "Did you have to hire someone else to complete the work?"
28. "What was your lost profit?"
29. "How do you calculate your damages?"
30. "What steps did you take to minimize losses?"
