## ‚ö° EXAMINATION QUICK TIPS

### Direct Examination - The SOAR Method
- **S**tart with credentials and foundation
- **O**rganize chronologically  
- **A**sk open-ended questions
- **R**ecap key points before finishing

### Cross Examination - The CRAFT Method
- **C**ontrol with leading questions
- **R**epeat favorable admissions
- **A**ttack when you have ammunition
- **F**ocus on one point at a time
- **T**erminate when you're ahead

## üí¨ ESSENTIAL QUESTION STARTERS

### Foundation Questions (Any Witness)
1. "Please state your full name for the record."
2. "What is your current address?"
3. "How are you employed?"
4. "Do you have any interest in the outcome of this case?"
5. "Have you discussed your testimony with anyone?"

### Timeline Development
6. "When did [event] first occur?"
7. "What were you doing just before [incident]?"
8. "What happened next?"
9. "How much time passed between [A] and [B]?"
10. "What did you do after [event]?"

### Sensory Details
11. "What did you see?"
12. "What did you hear?"
13. "What was the lighting like?"
14. "Who else was present?"
15. "How far away were you?"

### Document Foundation
16. "Do you recognize this document?"
17. "Have you seen it before?"
18. "What is it?"
19. "How do you recognize it?"
20. "Is this a true and accurate copy?"

## üéØ CASE-SPECIFIC QUICK REFERENCE

### Landlord-Tenant Cases (Edith Style)
**Key Questions:**
- "When did you first notice the problem?"
- "How did you report it to the landlord?"
- "What was their response?"
- "How did this affect your use of the property?"
- "When did you expect your deposit back?"

**Key Evidence:**
- Lease agreement
- Move-in/move-out photos
- Repair requests and communications
- Security deposit documentation
- Receipts for expenses

### Business Contract Cases (Concepts26 Style)
**Key Questions:**
- "What representations were made about their capabilities?"
- "When was performance supposed to be completed?"
- "How did their performance differ from what was promised?"
- "What additional costs did you incur?"
- "How do you calculate your damages?"

**Key Evidence:**
- Service contract/agreement
- Email communications
- Work product delivered
- Replacement contractor costs
- Industry standard documentation

## üé™ WITNESS HANDLING QUICK GUIDE

### Friendly Witnesses
- Use open-ended questions
- Let them tell their story
- Show documents to refresh memory
- End on strong points

### Hostile Witnesses
- Use leading questions only
- Keep questions short and simple
- Don't let them ramble
- Use their prior statements against them

### Expert Witnesses
- Establish credentials first
- Use hypothetical questions
- Make complex topics simple
- Connect opinions to case facts

## üó£Ô∏è CLOSING ARGUMENT TEMPLATE

### Structure (15-20 minutes)
1. **Introduction** (2 minutes)
   - "Your Honor, the evidence has shown..."
   - Restate your theme

2. **Evidence Summary** (10-12 minutes)
   - "First, we proved..."
   - "Second, the evidence showed..."
   - "Third, defendant failed to..."

3. **Law Application** (3-4 minutes)
   - "Under [applicable law]..."
   - "The elements have been met..."

4. **Damages** (2-3 minutes)
   - "My client has suffered..."
   - "The calculation is..."

5. **Conclusion** (1 minute)
   - Return to theme
   - Call for action
   - "Justice requires..."

## üé≠ PSYCHOLOGICAL TIPS

### Reading the Judge
- **Engaged:** Leaning forward, asking questions, taking notes
- **Convinced:** Nodding, focused attention
- **Skeptical:** Interrupting, challenging questions
- **Bored:** Looking away, checking time
- **Annoyed:** Sharp tone, cutting off arguments

### Adjusting Your Strategy
- **If winning:** Stay the course, don't over-argue
- **If losing:** Focus on strongest points, concede weak ones
- **If unclear:** Ask clarifying questions, check understanding

### Stress Management
- **Deep breathing:** 4 counts in, 4 counts hold, 4 counts out
- **Positive visualization:** See successful outcome
- **Physical grounding:** Feel feet on floor, hands on table
- **Confidence mantras:** "I am prepared," "I know this case"
